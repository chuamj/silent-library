At Silent Library, we are committed to protecting the privacy and security of our user information. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your data when you visit our website or use our online services

We may collect the following types of information:
  • Personal Information:
    • Name, email address, phone number, library card number
    • Mailing address (if applicable for library services)
    • Usernames and passwords for account access
  • Non-Personal Information:
    • Browser type, IP address, device information
    • Website usage data (pages visited, time spent, search queries)
    • Cookies and tracking technologies (see Section 4)
  • Patron Records:
    • Borrowing history, holds, fines (handled in accordance with library confidentiality policies)

We use collected information for:
  • Providing library services (e.g., account management, digital lending)
  • Improving website functionality and user experience
  • Communicating about library events, updates, or overdue items
  • Complying with legal obligations (e.g., law enforcement requests under applicable laws)

We do not sell or rent patron information. Data may be shared with:
  • Third-party service providers (e.g., catalog systems, e-book platforms) under strict confidentiality agreements
  • Legal authorities only if required by law (e.g., subpoena, court order)
  • Anonymous analytics to improve services (no personally identifiable information shared)

Our website may use cookies to:
  • Enhance user experience (e.g., saved preferences)
  • Analyze traffic patterns via tools like Google Analytics (opt-out available)
  • Manage login sessions for online accounts
You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but some features may not function properly.

We implement industry-standard security measures, including:
  • Encryption for sensitive transactions (e.g., online payments)
  • Secure servers and restricted staff access to personal data
  • Regular security audits

We comply with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). Parents/guardians may review or delete data for children under 13 by contacting us.

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may:
  • Access, correct, or delete your personal information
  • Opt out of marketing communications
  • Request data portability (where applicable)
Contact us at [Email/Phone] to exercise these rights.

We may update this policy periodically. Changes will be posted on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date.
